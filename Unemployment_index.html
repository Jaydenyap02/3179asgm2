Global Unemployment Rate

What is Unemployment Rate?

The unemployment rate is a key indicator of economic health. It represents the percentage of the labor force that is unemployed and actively seeking work. It provides insight into the economic challenges faced by a country and the ability of its labor market to absorb new and existing workers.

The goal of this visualization is to explore how unemployment rates have evolved globally between 1990 and 2021, highlighting countries with significant changes in unemployment, the average unemployment rates across different regions, and the relationship between human development and unemployment rates.

Unemployment Rate Around the World

How has unemployment changed globally?

Over time, unemployment rates have fluctuated across the globe due to various economic, political, and social factors. In many developed nations, unemployment rates remained relatively low and stable throughout the 1990s and early 2000s. However, events such as the 2008 global financial crisis led to a sharp rise in unemployment worldwide, with a slow recovery in many regions.

The COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 triggered another significant spike in unemployment rates as lockdowns and restrictions led to widespread job losses, especially in industries like tourism, retail, and manufacturing.

Analysis of Top 5 Countries

This graph highlights the five countries with the largest increases in unemployment rates from 1991 to 2021. Armenia saw the greatest rise, with unemployment growing from 0% to 19.3%. West Bank and Gaza followed, reaching 13.3%. Costa Rica, Botswana, and Panama also experienced notable increases, with Panama showing the slowest growth, rising from 8.4% to 10.9%.
